# Code of Conduct

---

## The Short Version

Be decent to each other. StackRoom is a project built by developers for developers. Everyone is welcome regardless of experience level, background, or where they are from. If you are here to build something useful and work with others in good faith, you belong here.

---

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful in issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Give honest and constructive feedback — attack the code, not the person
- Accept feedback on your own work without taking it personally
- Help others when you can, especially those newer to the codebase
- Acknowledge when you are wrong or do not know something
- Stay on topic in issues and discussions

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, or hostile language toward anyone
- Dismissing someone's contribution because of their experience level
- Harassment of any kind, public or private
- Posting others' private information without permission
- Deliberately derailing discussions or wasting maintainers' time
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project, or off-topic noise

---

## Scope

This applies everywhere the project exists — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other space where StackRoom is being discussed or worked on.

---

## If Something Goes Wrong

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, contact the maintainer directly and privately. Reports will be handled with discretion. Retaliation against anyone who reports a problem in good faith will not be tolerated.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers have the right to remove comments, close issues or PRs, and block contributors who do not follow this code of conduct. The goal is not to punish — it is to keep the project a place where people actually want to contribute.

---

## Final Note

This is a small open source project. Nobody is getting paid. Everyone here is spending their own time. Treat people accordingly.
